03 Hook
🎤 A simple test

"Let's build a mobile app for X."

That sentence is a solution looking for a problem.
Six months later the app ships. Nobody uses it.
Because nobody asked — should we have built this at all?

The BRD answers that question. Before a single developer is hired.

04 What a BRD is (and isn't)

Pre-solution · business language only

✅ A BRD is…
  • Pre-solution — no tech named
  • Business-focused — problem · users · outcomes
  • Stakeholder-ready — sponsors · leadership sign off
  • Solution-agnostic — software / process / policy
  • The funding document — gets budget approved
❌ A BRD is NOT…
  • A design document
  • A functional / technical spec
  • A project plan
  • A user manual
  • A place to say "we'll build a mobile app"

"The business needs the ability to…" is a BRD sentence. "The mBoB app should have a favourites screen" is a spec sentence — already a solution.

05 The BRD → PRD → SRS chain

Each document answers a different question

📋 BRD

"Should we do this at all?"

Audience: sponsor · leadership · steering
Language: business only
📊 PRD

"What product / release will we build?"

Audience: product · eng · design · GTM
Language: product + user
📄 SRS / Feature Spec

"How will we build this exactly?"

Audience: dev · QA · audit
Language: formal / technical

Traceability goes up too: FR-01 → BR-02 → BO-01. When someone asks "why are we building this?" — the answer is a chain, not a developer's memory of a meeting.

08 § 06 · Business Requirements · capability, not feature

"The business needs the ability to ___"

Format

BR-01 · The business needs the ability to [capability], so that [business outcome]. BR-02 · The business needs to [comply with / avoid / reduce X], so that [outcome].

Examples (Biller Favourites)

  • BR-01 — The business needs the ability for customers to mark a recently-paid biller as recurring, so that future payments to that biller are faster.
  • BR-02 — The business needs the ability for customers to retrieve recurring billers with one interaction, so that payment time drops.
  • BR-03 — The business needs to remain compliant with RMA §7 on data residency, so that no regulatory breach occurs.
  • BR-04 — The business needs MPIN re-authentication on every payment regardless of origin, so that security is not compromised.
09 § 04 · Problem statement · the anti-solution rule

If you name a solution, rewrite

❌ Solution-laden
  • "We need a mobile app for bill payments"
  • "Customers need a favourites feature"
  • "We should integrate with Core Banking API"
  • "Build a chatbot for support"
✅ Problem only
  • "Customers re-type 12-digit account numbers 36× per year each — 38 seconds per payment, 6% error rate"
  • "Support receives 12k tickets/month on self-serve questions — ~800 agent-hours lost"
  • "Compliance reporting takes 14 staff-days/month manual compile, 3% error rate"

The test: could this problem be solved in ≥ 2 ways? If only one solution fits, you've already written the solution.

10 The problem-statement checklist

Six questions a sponsor will ask

  • 🧍 Who is affected? Segment · size.
  • 💥 What concretely hurts? Described in observable detail.
  • 💰 How much does it cost today? Hours · BTN · tickets · churn · risk.
  • 📊 What's the evidence? Analytics · research · incidents.
  • ⏰ Why now? What changed · what's the trigger.
  • ⚠️ What happens if we do nothing? Baseline trajectory.

13 The delta = the value proposition

Quantify the gap before someone else argues it

Average payment time65 s → 20 s · −69%
Typing load (recurring)12 digits → 0 · eliminated
First-try error rate6% → ≈0% · eliminated
Support tickets on "save billers"8% vol → <1% · −87%
Per-customer time reclaimed (annual)≈27 min/yr

Every row here must trace to a success metric in §13 — and a measurement method that's instrumented before launch.

14 § 07 · Stakeholders · Interest / Influence grid

Engagement is driven by the 2×2

High interest · High influence

Manage closely — weekly check-ins, in every key decision.

Sponsor · Product Lead · Compliance

High interest · Low influence

Keep informed — fortnightly updates, include in demos.

Support team · Branch agents

Low interest · High influence

Keep satisfied — key decision points only, don't over-engage.

Finance · Legal · CTO

Low interest · Low influence

Monitor — monthly newsletter, no individual engagement.

15 RACI — and the SELISE RCI adaptation

Who does what · industry RACI + SELISE RCI

RACI (industry standard)

  • Responsible — the person(s) doing the work
  • Accountable — single owner of the outcome
  • Consulted — input sought before decision
  • Informed — kept in the loop after decision

RCI (SELISE adaptation)

Julian's insight: "Accountable" has no clean German equivalent and confuses teams. SELISE drops A and requires R to be exactly one person.

Teach both. Use RACI with external partners; RCI internally.

Regardless of which you use: every row of the matrix has exactly one Responsible party. If two people are R, nobody is.

17 § 14 · Cost-benefit · three parts

Wish list vs business case — the sign-off gate

A · Costs

Build (effort × rate) · Run (hosting · support · licences) · Change-mgmt (training · rollout comms)

B · Benefits

Quantified revenue · quantified cost savings · risk reduction · qualitative (brand · morale · strategic)

C · Payback

Break-even month · 3-yr NPV · sensitivity (what if adoption is 50% of target?)

19 Worked example · mBoB Biller Favourites BRD

§ 2 · Executive Summary (excerpt)

Retail customers of mBoB paying recurring bills face significant friction — re-entering 12-digit account numbers 36× per year. This initiative proposes a capability that lets customers save and reuse recurring payees with a single tap, reducing payment time by ≥70% and support-ticket load on "save billers" by >80%. Build cost BTN 2.4M · break-even month 9 post-launch.

§ 6 · Business Requirements (selected)

  • BR-01 — ability for a customer to mark a recently-paid biller as recurring
  • BR-02 — ability to retrieve recurring billers with one interaction
  • BR-03 — remain compliant with RMA §7 on data residency
  • BR-04 — MPIN re-authentication on every payment regardless of origin

Notice: not a single mention of "app", "screen", "favourites UI", "database". Pure capability.
