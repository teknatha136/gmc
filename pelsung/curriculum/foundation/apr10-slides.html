06 Communication Types

3 Types You Use Every Day

🗣️ Verbal

  • Speak at a pace others can follow
  • Avoid filler words (um, like, basically)
  • Confirm: "Does that make sense?"
  • Volume & tone matter

✍️ Written

  • One idea per sentence
  • Lead with most important info
  • Bullet points for lists
  • Shorter is almost always better

👁️ Non-Verbal

  • Eye contact = engaged & confident
  • Open posture = approachable
  • Nodding = active listening
  • Response time = urgency signal

🎯 Choose the right channel: Urgent & quick → Chat · Documented decision → Email · Nuanced/emotional → Call

08 Email Etiquette · 10:45
Email Etiquette

The Rules of Professional Email

📝 Subject Line

  • Bad: "Hi" / "Question" / "Important!!!"
  • Good: "[Action needed] API docs by Apr 15"
  • Use tags: [FYI] [Action needed] [Decision]
  • Under 60 characters. Never blank.

👥 To / CC / BCC

  • To: People who must act
  • CC: People who need to know
  • BCC: Sensitive situations only
  • Reply-All = only if everyone needs it

✅ Always Include

  • A greeting (Hi [Name],)
  • A clear ask or purpose
  • A deadline if relevant
  • A sign-off (Thanks, [Name])

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • 300-word essays for simple asks
  • "Per my last email..." (passive-aggressive)
  • Sending while angry — draft, walk away, re-read
  • CC-ing manager as a power move

A Well-Structured Email

From:Karma Wangchuk <karma@selise.ch>

12 Time Management · 11:35
Time Management

Where Does Your Time Actually Go?

  • Every morning you wake up with 24 hours — same as everyone else, no exceptions
  • The difference is not having more time — it's knowing what to say no to
  • Most wasted time isn't laziness — it's unclear priorities and digital distraction
  • In a software team, your wasted time = someone else's blocked task
  • The goal: protect deep work time like it's a meeting you can never skip

17 What is AI? · 12:10
Artificial Intelligence

What is AI, Really?

Not robots. Not magic. Not a replacement for your brain.

💬

Language AI (LLM)

Reads and writes text. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. Trained on billions of documents.

🎨

Image AI

Generates or edits images. Midjourney, DALL·E, Stable Diffusion.

🎵

Audio / Video AI

Transcribes, generates, or edits voice and video. Whisper, Suno, Runway.

🧠 An LLM is a very sophisticated autocomplete —
it predicts the most likely next word, over and over, at massive scale.

18 ChatGPT vs Claude

The Two Tools You'll Use

💚 ChatGPT

  • Made by OpenAI
  • Current flagship: GPT-4o
  • Most widely known worldwide
  • Has web search (paid tier)
  • Great for: writing, coding, general tasks
  • Free tier available

🟤 Claude

  • Made by Anthropic
  • Current flagship: Claude Sonnet 4
  • Designed to be safer & more careful
  • Very long context window (large docs)
  • Great for: coding (Claude Code), long documents, nuanced reasoning
  • Free tier available

💡 Which to use? Both are excellent — in this program we'll lean on Claude, especially Claude Code for software development work.

The Art of Prompting

Anatomy of a Great Prompt

prompt.txt
ContextI am a software developer at a company building a digital ID system for Bhutan.
TaskWrite a professional email to a client explaining a 2-week delay in the API delivery.
FormatUse a formal but empathetic tone. Keep it under 150 words. Include a subject line.
ExampleSubject line style: "[Project name] Update — [topic]"
Context — who/what/where you are Task — exactly what you want Format — how you want it Example — optional, very powerful
21 4 Rules

4 Rules of Great Prompting

1

Give it a Role

"You are a senior UX designer reviewing a mobile app..." Role-setting shifts the quality and perspective dramatically.

2

Be Specific About Output

Vague: "Write about APIs." Specific: "Write a 3-paragraph explanation of REST APIs for a non-coder using analogies."

3

Iterate — Don't Settle

AI is a conversation. If the first response isn't right: "Make it shorter and less formal." Keep refining in the same chat.

4

Give Examples

"Here's an example of the style I want: [paste]. Now write something similar about [topic]." This is few-shot prompting.

24 AI Ethics · 13:30
⚖️ AI Ethics & Responsible Use

AI Dos & Don'ts

✅ Do

  • Use AI to draft, then edit it yourself
  • Verify facts — especially numbers & dates
  • Use AI to learn — ask it to explain, not just answer
  • Treat it as a thinking partner, not a replacement for thinking
  • Tell your team when you've used AI to produce something

❌ Don't

  • Paste confidential client data into public AI tools
  • Submit AI output without reviewing it first
  • Blindly trust code AI writes — always read & test it
  • Assume AI knows the latest news — it has a training cutoff
  • Share passwords, personal IDs, or sensitive info in prompts

🔨 AI is a power tool, not a cheat code.
A carpenter with a nail gun is 10× more productive — but they still need to know where to put the nails.
Your job: know what to build. AI helps you build it faster.
