06 Communication Types

3 Types You Use Every Day

🗣️ Verbal

  • Speak at a pace others can follow
  • Avoid filler words (um, like, basically)
  • Confirm: "Does that make sense?"
  • Volume & tone matter

✍️ Written

  • One idea per sentence
  • Lead with most important info
  • Bullet points for lists
  • Shorter is almost always better

👁️ Non-Verbal

  • Eye contact = engaged & confident
  • Open posture = approachable
  • Nodding = active listening
  • Response time = urgency signal

🎯 Choose the right channel: Urgent & quick → Chat · Documented decision → Email · Nuanced/emotional → Call

08 Email Etiquette
Email Etiquette

The Rules of Professional Email

📝 Subject Line

  • Bad: "Hi" / "Question" / "Important!!!"
  • Good: "[Action needed] API docs review"
  • Use tags: [FYI] [Action needed] [Decision]
  • Under 60 characters. Never blank.

👥 To / CC / BCC

  • To: People who must act
  • CC: People who need to know
  • BCC: Sensitive situations only
  • Reply-All = only if everyone needs it

✅ Always Include

  • A greeting (Hi [Name],)
  • A clear ask or purpose
  • A deadline if relevant
  • A sign-off (Thanks, [Name])

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • 300-word essays for simple asks
  • "Per my last email..." (passive-aggressive)
  • Sending while angry — draft, walk away, re-read
  • CC-ing manager as a power move

12 Time Management
Time Management

Where Does Your Time Actually Go?

  • Every morning you wake up with 24 hours — same as everyone else, no exceptions
  • The difference is not having more time — it's knowing what to say no to
  • Most wasted time isn't laziness — it's unclear priorities and digital distraction
  • In a software team, your wasted time = someone else's blocked task
  • The goal: protect deep work time like it's a meeting you can never skip
