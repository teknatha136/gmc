05 Software Roles

Every Role You'll Hear About

🧭 BA / PM

Business Analyst / Product Manager — defines what to build & why.

🎨 UX / Designer

Designs how it looks & feels. Mockups, flows, design system.

💻 Frontend Dev

Builds what the user sees — web or mobile UI.

⚙️ Backend Dev

Builds the invisible engine — APIs, databases, logic.

🧪 QA / PAA

Quality Assurance / Product Acceptance Analyst — tests that it works.

🏗️ Architect

Designs the overall system shape & big technical decisions.

🔧 DevOps / SRE / Platform

One role with many names — automates build/deploy, keeps production running, handles security, builds internal dev tools. Often one person.

📞 Client/Support

Talks to customers, handles issues, bridges dev and business.

📈 Data / Analyst

Turns usage data into insight.

07 Domain Flavours

Teams Shift Shape by Industry

Same base roles — but each domain adds its own specialists.

🏦 Finance / Banking

  • Compliance Officer
  • Risk Analyst
  • External Auditor
  • Heavy on docs & approvals

🏥 Health / Medical

  • Clinical Informatics Lead
  • Privacy Officer (HIPAA/GDPR)
  • Regulatory Affairs
  • Patient Safety Reviewer

⛓️ Blockchain / Crypto

  • Smart Contract Auditor
  • Protocol Engineer
  • Tokenomics Lead
  • Very heavy on security reviews

Example: SELISE's Swiss-Ski Store is e-commerce — small, lean team. A project for a Bhutan bank would need extra compliance and audit roles. Same skills, different supporting cast.

12 RACI · Industry Standard

What does RACI mean?

R — Responsible

The person doing the work. Can be multiple people.

A — Accountable

The person who owns the outcome. Always exactly one.

C — Consulted

Their input is needed before a decision. Two-way dialogue.

I — Informed

Kept in the loop after the decision. One-way.

RACI is the global industry standard — used in banks, hospitals, governments.

14 RCI · SELISE's Adaptation
SELISE way

We drop the "A"

Julian (our CEO) observed: in German, there's no clean distinction between "Responsible" and "Accountable". And in practice, R is always exactly one person. So why have both?

R — Responsible

The one person who owns the outcome AND does the work (or coordinates it). Always exactly one.

C — Consulted

Input needed before the decision.

I — Informed

Told after the decision.

Simpler. Clearer. Every task has exactly one person's name next to R. No finger-pointing about who owns what.

16 Wrap-up
🎯 Key Takeaways

What we covered today

  • ✅ Software is built by many roles — BA, PM, QA, Designer, FE, BE, Architect, DevOps, SRE…
  • ✅ Domain (finance, health, crypto) adds specialists on top of the base roles
  • ✅ Team size follows project stage — active = big, maintenance = small
  • ✅ RACI (R, A, C, I) is the global standard
  • ✅ RCI is SELISE's simpler version — one person per R, no "A"
  • ✅ In the AI era, the team doesn't disappear — it gets smaller, more senior, more T-shaped
