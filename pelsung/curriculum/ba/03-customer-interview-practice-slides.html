03 Recap · Open vs Closed
Quick Recap

Open questions open mouths.
Closed questions close them.

From last class — same idea, fresh examples.

✗ Closed — yes / no / one word
  • "Do you use mBoB?"Answer: "Yes." End of trail.
  • "Is the bus schedule okay?"You learn nothing useful.
  • "Are you happy with your bank?"Most people say "yes" to be polite.
✓ Open — invites a story
  • "Walk me through the last time you sent money on mBoB."You hear the whole journey.
  • "Tell me how you usually plan a bus trip to Thimphu."You hear what they actually do.
  • "What's the most frustrating part of banking for you?"Now we're getting somewhere.
04 Recap · Problem-language & "Tell me about…"

Two habits that change every interview

✗ Solution-language
  • "Should we add a bigger Send button?"You're guessing the fix. Skip the problem.
  • "Would an SMS reminder help?"Customer says "sure" — means nothing.
  • "Do you want a simpler login?"Everyone wants simpler. Tells you zero.
✓ Problem-language & stories
  • "Tell me about a time you tried to send money and it didn't go through."Real moment. Real friction.
  • "What did you do last time you missed a bill payment?"You hear the workaround.
  • "Walk me through the last time you used the bank app."Step-by-step truth.

🎯 Golden phrase for today: "Tell me about a time when…" — use it whenever you feel the conversation drifting into hypotheticals.

05 The Interview Arc
A 20-minute interview has shape

Warm-up → Explore → Dig → Wrap

Don't open with the hardest question. Build trust, then go deep.

Phase 1
👋 Warm-up

Smile. Introduce yourself. Explain why you're talking to them. Ask one easy life question — "How was your morning?"

~2 min
Phase 2
🌅 Open exploration

Big, broad questions. Let them talk. "Tell me about a typical day at the shop." Don't interrupt. Don't pitch ideas.

~5 min
Phase 3
🔍 Focused dig

Pick the most painful thing they mentioned and follow it. "You said the QR code never scans — when did that last happen?"

~10 min
Phase 4
🤝 Wrap & summary

Read back 2–3 things you heard. "So the biggest pain was X — did I get that right?" Thank them. Ask if you can follow up.

~3 min
06 Listening for Pain Points
No customer says "I have a pain point"

Listen for these 4 signals instead

😤 Emotion

Sighs, frustration, "ugh", a little laugh of defeat. "Oh, mBoB? Don't even start me."

🪨 Friction

Moments where they slow down, repeat themselves, or describe doing the same step twice. "Sometimes I have to log in three times before it works."

🛠 Workarounds

Anything they invented to cope. Spreadsheets, paper notebooks, calling a relative for help. "I write the bus times in my diary because the app keeps crashing."

📣 Complaints & comparisons

"It used to be better when…", "My friend's bank does it differently", "I gave up on it." "I just go to the branch now."

When you hear any of these — slow down and dig. That's the gold.

08 Two-Column Note Method
Notes that survive the room

Left column: their words. Right column: yours.

Write a customer's actual words on the left — exactly. Your observations and follow-ups go on the right.

📓 Interview · Pema · 2026-04-22 · mBoB
🗣 Verbatim phrases
  • "It always asks me to log in twice."
  • "My daughter does it for me, actually."
  • "I don't trust the QR thing."
  • "I wrote my password on a paper inside my purse."
👀 Observations & follow-ups
  • Body language: sighed and looked away when describing login
  • Contradiction: said "I use it daily" then said "my daughter does it"
  • Workaround: paper password — security risk worth digging into
  • → Follow-up: ask when the double-login started

Capture verbatim quotes, body language, contradictions, and a follow-ups list. Don't try to write everything — write what surprises you.

Round 1
The mBoB First-Timer

Scenario

👵 An elderly person trying to use mBoB for the first time

Your customer is in their 60s, lives in Gelephu, has a smartphone their son set up. They've never used a banking app — but their pension is now coming through it. They want to send Nu. 500 to their grandchild for losar. Interviewer: find out what's hard, what's confusing, and what they actually do when stuck.

Role 1
🎤 Interviewer
  • Warm-up → explore → dig → wrap
  • Open questions only
  • "Tell me about a time…"
  • Don't pitch solutions
Role 2
🧓 Customer
  • Stay in character — be honest, a bit confused
  • Don't volunteer everything; make them ask
  • Use real Bhutanese context if you can
Role 3
📓 Observer / Notetaker
  • Two-column notes
  • Tally leading questions
  • Spot one missed follow-up

13 Group Debrief
🎤 Whole class · ~8 min

Three questions. One trio at a time.

😯
What surprised you?

A pain point you didn't expect. A workaround you'd never imagined. A quote that stuck.

😬
What felt awkward?

Did silence feel uncomfortable? Did you slip into solution-mode? Did a leading question sneak out?

✨
What worked?

One question that opened the customer up. One follow-up that paid off. One observation the notetaker caught.

Trainer: take 2–3 trios maximum. Pull out one verbatim quote per trio if possible.
